Course Details

All of our courses are taught by top-of-the-class tutors in their respective subjects, from published researchers to book authors to competition champions to musical prodigies. Our tutors are carefully vetted; each has a uniquely powerful teaching style and exhibits success in previous tutoring endeavors. All of us, although we take the hardest courses and the most difficult research, are joined in our passion for passing on knowledge to students just like us: people who truly want to learn.

Our courses are held both online over Zoom and in-person at Harvard Yard. The only exception is the college counseling and college essay programs, which are held exclusively online for the purpose of fairness. Although in-person courses are the same price as their online course counterparts, space is limited and these courses are first-come, first-serve. Furthermore, online courses will have the benefit of receiving class recordings; in-person courses will not.

Courses will typically be an hour to two hours per session, with homeworks and class recordings (if online) sent after the conclusion of the course. All necessary resources for the course, from practice problems done in-class to the homeworks, will be covered by the listed tutoring expense. There is no need for students to buy practice books, bring questions from class, or purchase any materials apart from perhaps a pencil, paper, and calculator.

The location of in-person courses at Harvard will be at the discretion of the tutor and decided at the time of registration. Locations for in-person courses will always be at or around Harvard Square. For reference, please see the map in the contacts page.
